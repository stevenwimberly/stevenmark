Research

At MIT's Plasma Science and Fusion Center, I worked on Spin-Orbit Torque (SOT) devices, a class of magnetic switching mechanisms with applications in next-generation non-volatile memory and neuromorphic computing. SOT exploits the spin Hall effect to switch the magnetization of a ferromagnetic layer using an in-plane current, enabling faster and more energy-efficient switching than conventional Spin-Transfer Torque (STT) devices.

My work involved the full fabrication pipeline: depositing multilayer thin films via e-beam evaporation, patterning devices using optical lithography and photoresist processing, and performing electrical characterization including anomalous Hall effect measurements and magnetoresistance sweeps on a Physical Property Measurement System (PPMS) cryostat down to cryogenic temperatures.

This hands-on experience with nanoscale fabrication and precision measurement equipment gave me a strong foundation in experimental physics methodology and the intersection of materials science, condensed matter physics, and device engineering.
